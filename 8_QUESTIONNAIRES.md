# 8 Dating Compatibility Questionnaires

Based on the Deep Research Report: *The Science of Romantic Compatibility, Relationship Satisfaction, and Marriage Longevity*

---

## Design Principles Applied

1. **Behavioral items over abstract self-labels** — questions ask what you *do*, not what you *value*
2. **Scenario-based trade-offs** — real-world dilemmas reveal priorities
3. **Stress reactions measured** — how you behave under pressure is more predictive than ideals
4. **Relationship-specific perceptions** — perceived partner commitment, appreciation, and satisfaction are 2–3× more predictive than individual traits
5. **Weighted by relationship stage** — attachment and values for initial compatibility; communication, equity, and shared meaning for long-term success
6. **Dealbreaker identification** — non-negotiable mismatches flagged early
7. **Critical vs. non-critical items distinguished** — contempt, avoidant attachment, and core value mismatches are weighted more heavily

## Question Format Key

| Format | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **single** | Multiple choice — choose 1 answer |
| **multi** | Select all that apply |
| **slider** | Scale of 1–10 with labeled anchors |
| **range** | Numeric range (min–max) |
| **likert** | 5-point scale: Strongly Disagree → Disagree → Neutral → Agree → Strongly Agree |

## Scoring & Weighting Notes

### Critical Items (⚠️ CRITICAL)
These items are weighted more heavily in compatibility scoring. They measure:
- Attachment security patterns (Q1.1, Q1.4, Q1.13, Q1.17)
- Four Horsemen behaviors (Q2.1, Q2.3, Q2.6, Q2.7, Q2.8)
- Core value alignment (Q3.10, Q3.16)
- Emotional regulation under stress (Q4.2, Q4.4, Q4.14)
- Commitment mindset (Q5.2, Q5.4, Q5.7, Q5.12, Q5.16)
- Sexual communication and adaptation (Q6.3, Q6.8, Q6.15, Q6.18)
- Equity orientation (Q7.3, Q7.4, Q7.10, Q7.19)
- Responsiveness and appreciation (Q8.2, Q8.6, Q8.7, Q8.8, Q8.12, Q8.17)

### Dealbreaker Items (⚠️ DEALBREAKER)
These items identify non-negotiable mismatches that should be surfaced immediately:
- Children (Q3.3)
- Monogamy orientation (Q3.18)
- Religion importance (Q3.1, Q3.2)
- Political alignment (Q3.6)
- Substance use (Q3.9, Q7.6, Q7.7)
- Living preferences (Q3.15)
- Pets (Q3.22)
- Cultural/religious background requirements (Q3.25)
