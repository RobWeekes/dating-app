# Dating App Compatibility Questionnaires (8 Categories)

Based on the deep research report: *The Science of Romantic Compatibility, Relationship Satisfaction, and Marriage Longevity*

## Design Principles

These questionnaires follow evidence-based design:

1. **Behavioral items over abstract self-labels** — "When I feel criticized, I tend to..." is more predictive than "I value communication"
2. **Scenario-based trade-offs** — e.g., "If your career required relocating, how would you handle it?"
3. **Stress reactions, not just ideals** — how someone behaves under pressure reveals more than what they say they value
4. **Relationship-specific perceptions** — perceived partner commitment, appreciation, and sexual satisfaction are 2–3× more predictive than individual characteristics
5. **Weighted by relationship stage** — attachment and values matter most for initial compatibility; communication, equity, and shared meaning matter most for long-term success
6. **Dealbreaker identification** — some value mismatches (children, religion, substance use) are non-negotiable and should be identified early
7. **Critical vs non-critical distinction** — contempt, avoidant attachment, and value mismatches on core issues are much more consequential than preferences about hobbies or food

## Response Format Reference

- **single** — multiple choice, choose 1
- **multi** — select all that apply
- **slider** — scale of 1–10 with labeled anchors
- **range** — numeric range
- **likert** — 5-point agreement scale (Strongly Disagree · Disagree · Neutral · Agree · Strongly Agree)

## Matching Weight Reference

- **Critical** — high-weight for matching; major mismatches drive strong match penalties
- **Standard** — normal weight; shapes "fit" gradients and conversation prompts rather than hard filters
- **Stage weighting:** early matching emphasizes Categories 1 & 3; long-term prediction emphasizes Categories 2, 7, & 8; Categories 5 & 6 screen for dealbreakers at all stages

## Questionnaire 1: Attachment & Emotional Security

### What this measures
Attachment orientation (secure vs. anxious vs. avoidant), comfort with emotional closeness, trust capacity, fear of abandonment, emotional availability, responsiveness to partner distress, and pursue–withdraw tendencies—especially under ambiguity or stress.

### Research basis
- Adult attachment patterns (secure/anxious/avoidant): **Bowlby (1969/1982); Hazan & Shaver (1987); Mikulincer & Shaver (2007)**
- Attachment injury / pursue–withdraw cycle (EFT): **Johnson (2004)**
- Stress amplifies vulnerabilities (VSA): **Karney & Bradbury (1995)**

**Importance weight: HIGH for initial compatibility; HIGH for long-term success**

## Questionnaire 2: Communication & Conflict Management

### What this measures
How someone handles disagreement—especially Four Horsemen risk (criticism, contempt, defensiveness, stonewalling), repair attempts, harsh vs soft startup, ability to de-escalate, and maintenance of the 5:1 positive-to-negative ratio.

### Research basis
- Predictors of dissolution; Four Horsemen; repair; 5:1 ratio: **Gottman & Levenson (1992); Gottman (1994); Gottman & Silver (1999)**
- VSA model: adaptive processes buffer stress: **Karney & Bradbury (1995)**

**Importance weight: MODERATE for initial compatibility; VERY HIGH for long-term success**

## Questionnaire 3: Values & Life Goals

### What this measures
Core values alignment (religion, politics, ethics), life ambitions, desired lifestyle, gender roles, money attitudes, parenting philosophy, geographic rootedness, dealbreaker identification. Values show strong assortative mating (politics r = 0.58) and drive "perpetual problems" if mismatched.

### Research basis
- Values structure and assortative mating: **Schwartz (1992); Buss (1989)**
- Values predict long-term fit; relationship-specific predictors: **Joel & Eastwick (2020)**
- Money beliefs ("money scripts") and communication: **LeBaron-Black et al. (2025)**

**Importance weight: VERY HIGH for initial compatibility; HIGH for long-term success**
